3. ### Tips and Tricks
* #### Wire Prep
   * Strip wire carefully to avoid fraying or cutting individual strands
   * Ensure all strands are straight and grouped together before inserting into connector
   * Follow the natural orientation of the wire to minimize additional stress and torque once they are installed in the housing
   * Strip the wire so there is only about 1/4 cm of exposed wire
* #### Connnector and Housing Pairing
   * Verify which connector type pairs with which housing type
   * The male connector typically will attatch to the male(plug) housing and the female connector will connect to the female(receptical) housing. 
   * Note: The gendering feels uncoventional so the specific Molex kit you have could switch up which part is deignated as male and female.
* #### Crimping
   * Select the correct wire gauge setting on your crimper. This gauge will correspond to the wire gauge which should also correspond to the size of the connector (typically 20 AWG for most applications).
   * Before crimping make sure only the prongs that will hold the conductor and the insulator are within the crimping tool. Make sure to avoid crushing other parts of the connector which are essential for it fitting into the plasting housing.
   * After crimping make sure to give it a tug to make sure all connections are secure.  
   * #### Crimping steps
   * The prongs on the end will crimp onto the insulation to hold the wire in place and the prongs after those will crimp onto the exposed wire. Make sure each prong forms a secure connection only with their designated components.
   * Place the bottom of the connector onto the tip of the correct gauge tooth on the crimper, such that the prongs of the connector are oriented into the space between teeth.
   * Close crimper slowly, but not all the way, just untill you hear a click indicating the crimper is locked. Thread the wire into place making sure it does not frey. Crimp fully once the wire is in place.
   * The crimpers RIF has allow both insulator and conductor tabs to be crimped at the same time. Align the pin correctly in the crimper with the conducting pin on the slightly elivated portion of the crimper.
